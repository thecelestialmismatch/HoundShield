## F. DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT

Use for all 8+ booked calls. Run in order. Do not pitch. Qualify or disqualify.
Target call length: 15 minutes.

**Opening (30 seconds):**
"Thanks for the time. I'm talking to a handful of compliance officers at defense subcontractors about a specific AI tool problem under CMMC. I have five questions — takes about 12 minutes. If it's not relevant, I'll say so and let you go. Sound good?"

---

**Q1 — Named incident (qualification gate 1):**
"Walk me through the last time you discovered an employee had put sensitive or controlled information into an AI tool. What happened next, hour by hour?"

Listen for: Named incident with date, person, consequence.
Disqualify if: "We haven't had that happen." [Move on — not qualified.]
Qualify if: "Last month, [engineer] pasted [document type] into ChatGPT. We found out because…"

---

**Q2 — Proof of negative (the C3PAO problem):**
"How would you prove to your C3PAO assessor or your prime's security officer that CUI has not crossed into ChatGPT, Copilot, or similar tools in the last 90 days?"

Listen for: "We can't" or "We have no way to audit prompts."
Disqualify if: "Our policy prohibits it." [They have compliance theater, not proof.]
Qualify if: "We can't. The CASB shows domain hits but not what was pasted."

---

**Q3 — Existing workarounds (what's failed):**
"What have you already tried — policies, training, CASB rules, outright bans? Where did each one break down?"

Listen for: Two or more failed controls.
Disqualify if: Single control, no pain articulated.
Qualify if: "Blocked ChatGPT, but engineers use personal accounts. Trained the team, but nobody remembers at 11pm. CASB shows domain, not content."

---

**Q4 — Assessment deadline (urgency anchor):**
"When is your CMMC L2 assessment scheduled? What's the latest you can wait before you need AI tool controls locked down?"

Listen for: Assessment in next 12 months, with a specific date or quarter.
Disqualify if: "We have 18 months or more." [They'll wait.]
Qualify if: "Assessment is Q3 2026. We have maybe 4 months."

---

**Q5 — Personal skin in the game (career consequence):**
"If an AI tool incident surfaces six months from now — after your assessment passes — what's the first thing that goes wrong for you personally? For your company?"

Listen for: Personal liability, contract loss, career consequence.
Disqualify if: "Compliance would handle it." [Diffused accountability.]
Qualify if: "I'd have to explain to the C3PAO we misrepresented our controls. The prime could pull the contract. I could lose my job."

---

**Closing (if qualified on all 5):**
"Everything you've described matches exactly what I'm solving. I'd like to set up a 30-minute technical call to show you exactly how it works — specifically the part where you generate an assessor-ready evidence report that answers the 90-day question. [Offer calendar link.]"

"One more thing — who else in your network is dealing with this? I'm talking to compliance teams at 8–10 companies in the defense space right now. If there's someone you'd introduce me to, I'll make it worth their time."

## G. QUALIFICATION CRITERIA — ALL 5 REQUIRED

A prospect is qualified for pilot only if YES to all:

1. **Named incident** — Real event, date, person, consequence. Not hypothetical.
2. **Active failing workaround** — Tried 2+ controls (ban, CASB, training). All fail or incomplete.
3. **Assessment deadline <12 months** — Hard C3PAO appointment, not "thinking about it."
4. **Willingness to pay $10K+ / year** — Stated unprompted, or budget-approved CMMC line item exists.
5. **Peer introductions offered** — At end of call, offers to introduce 2+ peers with same problem.

If only 4 of 5: keep in pipeline, re-qualify in 30 days.
